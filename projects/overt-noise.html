Overt Noise

An Unfiltered Ambient Social Media

Overt Noise is a project exploring the possibility of using CubeSats to create a new kind of unfiltered and ambient social media. The project is a response to the current form of filtered and false social media (e.g. Facebook, Instagram) and provides a way to see a more real glimpse into other people's lives. It increases empathy between strangers by doing away with holiday posts, and Instagram filtered food photos and instead embrace the shared frailty and mundaneness of human life.

Read the Project Journal

Concept

This was a speculative project set in the near future when CubeSats (a satellite standard) have become cheaper and the instruments needed to communicate with them have become smaller. The project aims to challenge what we think of when talking about social networks and to examine the notion of what we find interesting in our own and other people's lives. Do we really need to see more photos of your food, or your babies face, or you on holiday? Or are we more interested in that argument you had with your mum or the time when you were doing your washing on a Saturday? I believe that to build empathy, we need to break the tradition of "Empire" and embrace the shared frailty and mundaneness of human life.

How it might work

There are two touch points of the project, The Overt Noise Listeners, a set of minimal jewellery pieces each equipped with a microphone and radio transmitter. The second touchpoint is the Overt Noise Speaker which plays audio transmitted by Overt Noise Listener. However, only Listeners directly under the CubeSat will transmit, with CubeSat rotating around the world and picking up bits of Peoples lives like a person walking down the street picks up moments of conversation. All of the communication between the touchpoints is done through radio waves on the CubeSats, with the system working as a closed loop, with no storing of audio or personal information.

The Overt Noise system would be financed by the sales of Speakers and the Listeners would be given out for free. Hence the Speaker has a high-end aesthetic and becomes a sort of sculpture as it traces the path of the satellite on a sandy base.
