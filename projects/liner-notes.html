Liner-Notes

A bespoke music magazine

Liner-Notes is a response to the way people listen to music in the age of digital streaming. This new way of music consumption has given rise to the loss of a person's music collection, an individualised experience of music when there used to be a sociable one, and an industry of underpaid artists. Liner-Notes attempts to change this by introducing a new quarterly bespoke music magazine based on the music you listen to. It is the record of your month in music and gives you a beautiful breakdown of the information behind the songs you value most.

Read the Project Journal

Research

As my final year's self-initiated project, we were given an open brief to design a service or artefact that was important to us. I've been passionate for years on 'individual music identity in the age of digital streaming', however, I was also aware that designing with only your opinions is a short road to frustration and failure, hence I wanted to push my own practice of collaboration, co-design and user research.

Through interviews and ethnography kits, I identified a commonality of music history of user aged between 20-25 (diagram below). This journey that started with CDs, Limewire and iTunes libraries ultimately ended with the new standard of music consumption; digital streaming. This has given rise to the loss of a person's music collection, an insular experience of music where there used to be a sociable one, and an industry of underpaid artists.

After gathering this research I quickly realised the only outlet that people had to really show they were music fans was flawed. Vinyl, despite its analogue interaction, has a high set up cost, requires space to display and use, and doesn't reflect the way we listen to music. Users listen to playlists, not albums, and buying an album not only limited that users expression but also only supported a single artist, not the plethora of artists users wanted to support. Concerts are also a bad answer to the question for much the same reason, but with the added difficulty of limited tour destinations. In the end, I was left with the question: what does it mean to say "I have that on Vinyl" in the age of digital streaming.

Development

Taking my research question and user insights, I developed a workshop to co-design a new artefact or service. Artists, fans and the wider music industry all took part in the workshop, culminating in a huge range of proposals, of which the key points were; the product should add a level of transparency to music consumption, it should work with existing streaming services and it should be physical in order to provoke sharing and conversation.

I settled on a mail out subscription service customised to each user's particular listening habits. To do this, AI would be key in creating bespoke products.

The basic overview of the service followed as such; a widget on your devices would track your plays each month. A designer would then design a unique layout for each month of the magazine. This would include a design for the cover of the magazine and for the inside pages which would provide information on your plays each month. This layout would be populated by an AI that would find information about your top songs each month. This information could be the lyrics from your favourite songs and the meanings behind them taken from Genius, a list of samples used in the song’s construction from Whosampled or even reviews from sites like Pitchfork. The magazine would be funded through a 50/50 subscription model with half of your subscription going towards the company and the other half going to the artists you listened to that month.

Outcome

The final product: Liner-Notes. A quarterly bespoke music magazine based on the music an individual user listens to. Not only does it become a record of the music that the user listens to each month, but it gives them a beautiful breakdown of the information behind the songs they value most. Each copy comes with a personal mixtape of their top songs so they can listen as they explore each song's lyrics, samples or meanings. The magazine layout is created by a different graphic designer each quarter and then populated with information about their top songs by Liner-Notes' in-house AI. This means when they display each issue on their wall or leave it on their coffee table, it will be sure to start a conversation. Better still it supports artists, by dividing half of the subscription fee between the artists they listen to most, weighted to support smaller artists more, and the service itself.

These quarterly magazines will be the almanacks of a users music life. So when a friend comes over, and they're discussing that road trip they took last June, they will be able to bring their last Summers' Liner-Notes down from the shelf and rediscover the songs that were the background to that trip.
