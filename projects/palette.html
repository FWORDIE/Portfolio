A Project About Bronies

For this project, I was tasked to design specifically for a subculture. My chosen subculture was Bronies, who are unexpected fans of My Little Pony. During the course of the project, I spent a huge number of hours at Brony meet-ups and conducting one on one interviews with members of the Glasgow Bronies Community. In the end, I co-designed with the subculture and created a tool to aid them in explaining to others why they like the show in the form of a high-end fashion brand based around their passion.

Read the Project Journal

Initial Research

ofAfter choosing to explore Bronies, a group of adults who were fanatical about the children’s TV show My Little Pony, I spent a number of hours doing very personal interviews, with the Brony community in Glasgow, as well as spending time at their weekly meet-ups. During the course  the project I gained intimate knowledge and understanding of the subculture not only through interviews but also through the use of photographic cultural probes. The pictures seen below are examples of these probes, that gave me a deeper insight into what went on in their weekly meetups when I was not present.

Development

After spending time with the community, I soon realised that they hated how Bronies were perceived both by the greater public and even by their friends and family. Ifor one felt bad for initially judging them and now I wanted to try to create a platform for bronies to express who they really are and more importantly who they aren’t. My idea was to create merchandise that was specifically aimed at bronies, something more subtle then what was offered at the moment. The items would double as a tool that would help the Bronies explain to people they met, why they loved the show.

After focusing on this specific area, I organised a co-design drawing workshop with the Glasgow community. I set them a number of challenges to not only gain an idea of what aesthetics they liked, but also what references from the show I could use.  I soon realised that the aspect of the show that really drew them in was its pastel art style and that they all related to specific ponies from the show. Next I tried to explore how I could use these pony colour palettes effectively.

Outcome

My final outcome for this project was a series of items that used the colour palettes of various ponies in subtle ways. The range was intended to be subtle but at the same time recognisable to every My Little Pony fan. However, I also left little hints as to the origin of the colour palette, through having the corresponding ponies cutie mark on the item's label. I created a website and logo that showed the sophistication of the brand, I wanted it to be higher end fashion than the franchise’s usual merchandise. The hope with these items was that even we, the non-bronies, like them and might even comment on someone wearing one. This could inturn open a discussion about why they like the show, to which we would be more open to listening and more open to understanding. For starters we like the bronies shirt, why should we not like the show?
