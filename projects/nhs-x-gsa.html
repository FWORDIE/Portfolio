NHS x GSA

A NHS Staff Workshopping Kit

A live project with NHS Crosshouse Hospital, in which we were tasked with helping the NHS staff identify and problem solve issues within the hospital. The result was a staff workshopping kit that included tools to help them identify problems, then find the root of the issues and then use tools to come up with innovative and empathic solutions.

Read the Project Journal

Research

To generate a meaningful outcome we spent a considerable amount of time at the hospital. In Order to begin to understand the existing systems within the hospital and the patient journeys.

From this we generated many insights, these in turn were brought back to the hospital for the staff and patients to comment on. It became obvious from these insights and the comments, that only the people at the hospital would have the knowledge and understanding to solve these issues. Hence we decided to develop a tool kit that would enable the staff to identify issues, solve these and then implement solutions.

Development

One of the ways we decided to help the NHS staff was to create a document that would encapsulate not only all of our research but also our process. The most important part of this document was to create something digestible, unlike the type of NHS research papers that the staff were used to.

Outcome

Over the course of 4 weeks we set about designing engaging tools to help the NHS problem-solve. When developing them, we were unable to test them on NHS staff. We overcame this by testing them on university students who wanted to problem-solve issues in the university. This enabled us to understand how non-designers would approach the tools.

Our final set of tools consisted of:
‍
- An Identifying issue sheet that enabled NHS staff to quickly note down their expectation vs the reality of a problem.
‍
- A triangle of communication that would help them breakdown where the issues in communication were apparent.
‍
- A roleplaying game that would help the staff visualise issues and become more empathetic towards patients or other staff members.
‍
- Insight cards, as quick fire starting points for discussion.
‍
These tools were then presented to the staff at a workshop on how to use the various tools. After the workshop the project was presented at at conferences and we were invited back to hold another workshop using our tools.
